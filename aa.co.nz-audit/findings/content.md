**Experience (32/100):** Zero member testimonials, no case studies, no first-hand breakdown stories. Campaign character illustrations ("Ray", "Bob") instead of real members. "We rescue a Kiwi every minute" stat is unattributed.

**Expertise (58/100):** Service descriptions accurate and technically correct. Breakdown safety guide shows procedural expertise. AA Plus benefit limits clearly documented. No named author/editor credited anywhere.

**Authoritativeness (72/100):** Founded 1903, 300+ contractors, 1,000+ staff. 2026 Reader's Digest Most Trusted Brand award image present. Shielded Site trust badge. No external editorial mentions or third-party review aggregator scores.

**Trustworthiness (61/100):** Pricing transparent with Auckland vs Rest of NZ split. T&Cs linked throughout. Contact numbers on every page. However: missing meta descriptions, seasonal stale meta desc on join-aa, outdated EV content, no "last updated" dates.

## What Works
- Full loyalty discount table (Year 1–50+, Auckland vs Rest of NZ pricing)
- AA Plus benefit caps explicitly documented ($800/event, $1,500/year)
- Roadside join pricing disclosed ($209 or $159 with member partner)
- Roadservice coverage scope clearly enumerated including EV/bicycle
- 45+ member benefits with specific discount percentages
- Consistent footer contact info across all pages

## Critical Issues

**C1. Missing meta descriptions on 6 pages**
- /membership/, /standard-membership/, /youth-membership/, /roadservice-breakdown-assistance/, /renew/
- /membership/ is highest-traffic page in set — Google will auto-generate a potentially misleading snippet

**C2. Stale forward-looking claim on /aas-mobile-ev-charging/**
- "A fourth van will be introduced to Christchurch later in 2022" — 4-year-old unresolved claim
- /join-aa/ still says "coming soon to Christchurch" — contradicts any resolution
- Factual accuracy failure under QRG trustworthiness criterion; will be cited incorrectly by AI systems

**C3. /join-aa/ meta description is seasonally stale**
- "This summer, drive with confidence..." — misleading for ~9 months/year

**C4. Vague H1 on primary conversion page**
- /join-aa/ H1: "We've got you." — brand tagline, not descriptive; page targets highest-intent join query
